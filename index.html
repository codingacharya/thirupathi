Thesis Title

Predicting the Growth of Startup and Small-Cap Companies using Machine Learning

Research Supervisor

Prof. G. Shankar Lingam

Research Area
Machine Learning Artificial Intelligence Stock Market Prediction Startup Analytics Deep Learning Time Series Forecasting
Research Objectives
  • Developed a machine learning model to predict startup and small-cap company revenue growth.
  • Designed a framework to estimate startup survival probability using historical business indicators.
  • Built an intelligent valuation model to forecast startup valuation and possible exit strategy.
Methodology

The research utilized financial datasets collected from NSE and Yahoo Finance. Data preprocessing, feature engineering, and model training were performed using Python. Machine learning and deep learning models including Linear Regression, LSTM, CNN, and RNN were implemented for stock price prediction. Performance was evaluated using RMSE and F1-Score, while Power BI and Tableau were used for visualization and business insights.

Key Findings
  • Linear Regression achieved approximately 98% prediction accuracy.
  • LSTM effectively modeled sequential financial data for long-term forecasting.
  • The framework supports startup valuation and investment decision-making.
Research Novelty

This research proposes an integrated framework for predicting the growth, valuation, and survival of Indian startup and small-cap companies. It also considers macroeconomic factors such as inflation, RBI and FED interest rate changes, making the prediction model more practical for real-world investment analysis.

Technologies Used
Python Machine Learning LSTM CNN RNN AWS Power BI Tableau
Research Outcome
AI-Based Trading Bot

Developed an intelligent trading bot using Machine Learning and AWS for Futures & Options (F&O) trading. The system assists traders by providing data-driven trading decisions based on historical market behavior and predictive analytics.

🎓 PhD Thesis Citation

Predicting the startup and smallcap companies growth with machine learning

Researcher Kandadi Thirupathi Reddy
Guide / Supervisor Prof. G. Shankar Lingam
Department Computer Science and Engineering
University Chaitanya Deemed to be University, Hyderabad
Year of Completion 2026
Abstract

[Objectives: Create a model that predicts the revenue increase of a startup or small-cap firm during a specified timeframe, Develop a model that forecasts the probability of a startup enduring beyond a specific period, utilizing historical trends and risk signals and Create a framework that predicts a startup's forthcoming value and possible exit approach. Methodology: To predict the startup and small cap company’s growth with machine learning we have gathered multiple datasets of startup and small-cap companies from different sources such as NSE, Yfinance. Following the gathering of the raw data, we have refined the dataset using machine learning methods with Python programming. We have removed certain unnecessary data from the dataset and subsequently tested and trained the information using machine learning approaches. The LSTM algorithm has been selected to forecast the stock price of a company. This algorithm operates in conjunction with CNN and RNN within neural networks. We applied the Linear Regression and LSTM algorithms to predict the stock price of a company after testing and training the dataset. Then, we determined the accuracy utilizing R-MSE techniques. We evaluate the precision using the F1 score. To visualize the data we chosen the tools like powerBI and tableau in some cases. Findings: The Linear Regression algorithm achieves a remarkable accuracy of 98% in forecasting stock prices. When compared to alternative algorithms, Linear regression stands out as the most effective choice for stock price prediction. Other algorithms fail to deliver a higher level of accuracy in forecasting stock prices. Novelty: This research aims to identify the most effective algorithm for forecasting the stock price of a startup and small cap firms in India. Sometimes, it becomes quite challenging to forecast stock prices because of global economic factors such as CPI inflation figures in the USA, interest rate reductions from significant financial entities like the FED in the USA and RBI in India. These forecasts hold true when all economic and global circumstances are favorable. Keywords: Forecasting stock prices, LSTM model, Startup evaluation, CNN, RNN, Precision, R-MSE, F1 score in precision.]
